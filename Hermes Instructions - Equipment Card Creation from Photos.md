## Photo Intake Best Practices (Critical for High-Quality Cards)

When Darren shares photos of equipment, follow these guidelines to produce cards that are genuinely useful and visually rich:

### Photo Analysis Standards
- Examine every image carefully for:
  - Data plates / serial numbers / model numbers
  - Hour meter readings
  - Visible wear, damage, modifications, or aftermarket parts
  - Fluid levels, filters, belts, and service points (when visible)
  - Overall condition and context (where it is stored, attachments present, etc.)
- If text is visible in photos (even partially), transcribe it accurately.

### Image Placement & Organization
- Do **not** dump all photos at the bottom.
- Distribute photos logically throughout the card:
  - **Observe section**: Hero/overview shots + clear data plate close-up
  - **Learn / Key Information**: Photos of engine, transmission, deck/cutting system, controls, and any unique features
  - **Execute / Standard Procedures**: Photos of daily service points, safety switches, grease points, and common maintenance areas
  - **Notes & Log**: Photos of any current issues or wear patterns worth documenting
- Always include descriptive captions under images, e.g.:
  - `![Data plate - 2024 John Deere Z530R](photo.jpg)`
  - Caption: "Data plate showing model Z530R and serial number"

### When Photos Are Limited
- Clearly note what is missing (e.g., "No photo of hour meter was provided").
- Ask for specific additional photos only when they would significantly improve the card (e.g., "A close-up of the engine data plate would help confirm the exact model").

### Media Quality
- Prioritize clear, well-lit photos.
- If provided images are poor quality, note this and suggest better photos for key areas (data plate, service points, overall condition).

### Stock Photo Sourcing Rules (Mandatory)
When adding or suggesting photos for an Equipment Card (especially stock or reference images from the web):

- **Only use photos of the actual device/model** being documented.
- Never use generic, illustrative, or unrelated stock images (example: do not use a photo of a bear for a chainsaw card, even if it is "themed").
- If a real photo of the specific machine is not available, use:
  - Official manufacturer product photos of the exact model.
  - High-quality photos of the same make/model/year from reliable sources.
  - Clearly label any stock photos as "Representative of [Exact Model]" in the caption.
- Always prefer user-provided photos of the actual machine over stock images when possible.
- If using a stock photo, verify it matches the exact specifications (color, attachments, year, etc.) of the equipment being carded.

This rule exists to keep cards accurate and professional. Irrelevant or "cute" stock photos reduce trust and usability.

## Maintenance Schedule Format (Mandatory)

Use this exact structured format:

```markdown
## Maintenance Schedule

- **Task**: Engine Oil & Filter Change
  - Interval: Every 50 hours or annually (whichever comes first)
  - Last Completed: [Date] at [Hours]h
  - Current Hours (at last service): [Number]h
  - Next Due At: [Calculate based on interval]
  - 10% Window Starts At: [90% of interval before Next Due]
  - Notes: Use SAE 10W-30 or per manual. Always replace filter with oil change.
```

Include all major recurring tasks (oil changes, blade services, air filter, belts, winterization, etc.).

## Service Instructions Format (Mandatory)

Create detailed, step-by-step instructions under:

```markdown
## Service Instructions

### Engine Oil & Filter Change
1. Park on level ground...
2. ...
**Special notes for this machine:**
- ...
```

Write these as if the user is standing in the shop at the end of the day and needs clear guidance.
