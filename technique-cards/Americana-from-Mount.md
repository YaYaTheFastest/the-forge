# 🥋 Americana from Mount

> [!important] KEY CUE  
> “Elbow to ear” — drive their hand toward their own ear while keeping their elbow glued to the floor. Heavy hips, light arms.

---

## 1. Core Technique

**Name:** Americana (Keylock) from Mount  
**Belt Level:** Blue belt (fundamental submission)  
**Position / Entry:** Top mount control, typically after establishing high mount or following a failed armbar attempt. Can be entered from side control as a transition into mount or directly when opponent frames with elbow on mat.  
**Goal:** Isolate the shoulder joint using a figure-four grip and finish with chest pressure.

---

## 2. Key Principles

> [!tip] Principle 1 – Figure-Four Grip Leverage  
> Use the figure-four grip to create mechanical advantage. The opponent’s own arm acts as the fulcrum. Your chest and body weight finish the submission, not arm strength.  
> *Why it works*: Turns their defensive frame against them.

> [!tip] Principle 2 – High Mount Walk-Up  
> Walk your knees high toward their head before finishing. This removes their ability to bridge or shrimp effectively.  
> *Why it works*: High mount = maximum control and leverage.

> [!tip] Principle 3 – Wrist Pin Below 90°  
> Keep their elbow pinned to the mat with the wrist below shoulder level. Never let the elbow lift.  
> *Why it works*: Prevents them from creating space or rolling to the trapped side.

> [!warning] Common Failure Points  
> - Allowing them to roll to their side (must block with knee pressure)  
> - Losing the figure-four grip by letting the wrist slip  
> - Applying pressure upward instead of across toward their head  
> - Neglecting to control the head or far arm

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Execution

1. Establish strong mount with knees high and tight to their ribs/hips. Weight centered low.
2. Identify the target arm (usually the one they frame with). Pin their wrist to the mat with your same-side hand, keeping their elbow bent and below shoulder level.
3. Thread your opposite hand under their trapped arm and grab your own wrist (figure-four grip).
4. Walk your knees aggressively toward their head into high mount while maintaining the wrist pin.
5. Rotate your torso slightly toward the trapped side. Drive your elbows toward the mat while pulling their wrist upward toward their head.
6. Finish by pinching your elbows together and leaning your chest weight forward. Apply steady, increasing pressure across their body.
7. **Personal cue:** “Elbow to ear” — drive their hand toward their own ear while keeping their elbow glued to the floor. Use “heavy hips, light arms.”

---

## 4. Variations & Transitions

**Primary Variations:**
- High Mount Americana (walk even higher for more leverage)
- Americana from Side Control (enter before mounting)
- Reverse Americana / Kimura-style from mount

**Transitions From Here:**
- Armbar from Mount (if they roll hard to escape)
- S-Mount or Technical Mount (use the threat to force exposure)
- Counter to Bridge & Roll (use their momentum to spin into technical mount)

**Related Techniques:** Armbar from Mount, S-Mount Transition, Americana from Side Control

## ⚡ Quick Reference
- **Goal:** Isolate the shoulder with figure-four grip and finish with chest pressure
- **Core Movement:** High mount + wrist pin + figure-four
- **Success Look:** Opponent taps as shoulder reaches external rotation limit

## 🔥 Why This Matters
This is one of the highest-percentage submissions from mount. It builds top pressure awareness and teaches you to use leverage instead of strength. Mastering it makes your entire mount game more dangerous.

## 🏋️ Drills (Short & Specific)

**Drill 1 – Wrist Pin & Grip (4 min)**  
Partner in mount. Repeatedly establish the wrist pin and figure-four without finishing. Focus on speed and control.

**Drill 2 – High Mount Walk-Up (4 min)**  
From mount, practice walking knees high while maintaining wrist pin. Partner tries to bridge and shrimp.

**Drill 3 – Live Finish (6 min)**  
Full technique from live mount. Reset on escape or tap. Focus on retention during the finish.
